02 · What you get

the complete architectural set.

Every architecture engagement at BloxDot includes the same baseline deliverables, scaled to the project. Detail and depth increase with project scope. Nothing critical is left out, regardless of size.

concept design

Site analysis report. Massing studies. Two design directions presented together. Mood and material direction. One round of revisions included.

schematic design

Plans, elevations, sections at 1:100 scale. Refined material palette. Spatial layout locked. 3D massing visualization.

design development

Plans and sections at 1:50 scale. Construction details for key conditions. MEP coordination drawings prepared with our engineering partners. Specifications document.

construction documents

Full set at 1:50 and 1:20. Detail drawings for joinery, finishes, fixtures. Door and window schedules. BOQ-ready specifications. Approval submission set.

handover package

Final stamped drawings. Digital files in DWG and PDF. As-built coordination if BloxDot is also building.

03 · How it runs

five phases, scaled to the project.

Project size sets the timeline. A villa runs longer than an apartment, a commercial shell longer than a renovation. The five phases below are constant. Depth and duration scale with scope.

01
Phase

brief & site study

Two-week phase. We walk the site, study the brief, return with a one-page direction document and timeline.

02
Phase

concept design

Three-to-four-week phase. Two design directions, mood, materials, massing. The big decisions are made here.

03
Phase

schematic design

Four-to-six-week phase. Plans, sections, elevations refined. Spatial layout locks. Material palette confirmed.

04
Phase

design development

Six-to-ten-week phase. Construction-grade detailing. MEP coordination. Engineering partners brought in.

05
Phase

construction documents

Six-to-eight-week phase. Full set ready to build from. Approval submission. BOQ-ready specifications.

Typical residential: four to six months end to end. Commercial: six to nine months. Renovation: two to four months. We confirm a project-specific timeline at sign-on.

05 · Design only, build, or supervise

three ways to engage us.

Most architecture clients pick one of three paths. Design only, where we hand over a complete drawing set and you take it to a contractor of your choice. Design and build, where BloxDot is the contracted general contractor under one agreement. Design and supervise, where you hire a contractor of your choice and we oversee the build to protect the design.

For design-only engagements, our drawings are detailed enough to build from without coming back for clarifications. For design and build, the studio that drew the project is the team running the build. For design and supervise, the architect who drew it watches it get built, without taking over construction.

06 · Questions buyers ask

what comes up on most first calls.

The architecture questions we hear most often, answered directly.

How long does an architectural design take in Pakistan?

+

A residential project takes four to six months from signed brief to construction-ready drawings. Commercial projects run six to nine months. Renovations are faster, two to four months. The timeline depends on project scale, approval requirements in your jurisdiction, and how quickly the client returns decisions on concept and materials.

What does an architect deliver in Pakistan?

+

A complete architectural set. That means site plan, floor plans, elevations, sections, and detail drawings at the scales required for construction. It includes structural coordination with engineering partners, MEP coordination, door and window schedules, and a specifications document detailed enough for a contractor to price from. Anything less is incomplete.

What makes BloxDot different as an architect in Lahore?

+

Sohaib leads design on every project personally, from concept through construction documents. The same principal who takes the brief is the principal who reviews the final drawing set. We also offer construction as a core service, which means clients can engage us for design only or for design and construction together under one contract.

Do I need an architect if I already have a contractor?

+

Yes for any project that needs permit approval, structural modifications, or meaningful spatial changes. A contractor builds. An architect decides what gets built, how it works spatially, and how the structure handles loads, light, and movement. Without proper architectural drawings, contractors estimate from incomplete information, which is where most cost overruns and design failures originate.

Can I hire BloxDot for design only?

+

Yes. Most of our architecture engagements end at the drawing set. We hand over a tender-ready package with structural and MEP coordination already resolved, and the client appoints a contractor of their choice. The set is detailed enough that a competent contractor can price and build from it without coming back for clarifications.

What does architectural design cost in Pakistan?

+

Architecture fees scale with built-up area, structural complexity, and the level of detailing a project demands. Residential villas typically run as a percentage of construction cost. Renovations and smaller homes are quoted as fixed fees. Commercial projects are scoped against a defined deliverable list. We send a written fee proposal after the first call. No blind estimates over the phone.

Do you handle approvals and permits?

+

We prepare the approval drawing set to the standards required by your local authority. The submission and follow-up with the authority is typically handled by the client or a permit consultant, with us coordinating closely on any technical clarifications. For complex projects, we can recommend permit consultants we have worked with before.

Can BloxDot work on a site outside Lahore?

+

Yes. Architecture travels well because most of the work is studio-based. We have run projects in Karachi, Islamabad, and Dubai from our Lahore studio. Site visits are scheduled around concept sign-off, drawing issue, and key construction milestones rather than a weekly cadence, so the travel cost stays proportional to the scope.
