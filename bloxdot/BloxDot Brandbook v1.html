I ——— LOGO

logo

A quiet wordmark set in a high-contrast display serif, closed by a single crimson dot. The dot is inherited from our parent studio, HybridDot — a promise of craft, and a rule that ends every title.

bloxdot
Primary lockup

The wordmark is the principal identifier. It is set in Fraunces Light at a tight letter-spacing of −0.05em and terminates in a solid crimson disc equal to the lowercase x-height.

Construction

Built on a 4-unit baseline grid. The four glyphs of blox and dot occupy equal optical width; the crimson disc is offset by 0.08 em and sits on the baseline.

Rendering

Use Fraunces variable font with opsz 144, SOFT 20. Do not use static weights below 300 — the mark should feel carved, not drawn.

Minimum size

Digital: 88 px wide. Print: 22 mm wide. Below this, use the monogram (B.) in its place.

Licensed usage

The mark is the property of BloxDot Studio. Third-party use requires written permission. Rendering in any other typeface constitutes misuse.

III. — Clear space
bloxdot x — clear space x

The x rule

Clear space around the wordmark equals the x-height of the lowercase letters. Nothing — type, imagery, other marks, page edges — may cross this boundary. The dot is part of the mark; it counts.

All sides
= 1× x-height
Preferred
= 1.5× x-height
Alignment
Baseline to grid baseline

Construction

The wordmark is plotted on a 4-unit × 9-unit grid with an x-height of 100 units. The crimson disc is a perfect circle; its diameter equals 0.58 × x-height and its center sits 0.08 em right of the final letter.

This geometric rigor is inherited from HybridDot and is non-negotiable: the system's integrity depends on the mark rendering identically at every size.

II ——— COLOR

color

A warm, material palette. Bone and plaster instead of paper-white. Ink instead of pure black. Brass for details. Crimson — inherited from HybridDot — appears only as a signature, never as a field.

Philosophy

BloxDot designs in material: lime plaster, oiled oak, unpolished travertine, oxidised brass. The palette is the same vocabulary translated into pigment. Nothing is pure; everything is honest.

Crimson · 04 · Signature

The one-percent rule

Crimson is used sparingly — the dot in a mark, a rule on a date line, an emphasized word. It should not exceed two percent of any composition. A little goes a long way; too much, and the system breaks.

  • The closing dot on every title — lowercase word, red dot.
  • A single accent on one word per page of body copy, for emphasis.
  • The hairline under a section number, or a tick on a diagram.
  • Never fill a large shape or background with crimson.
  • Never pair crimson directly against brass without ink between.
  • Never use any tint, shade, or gradient of crimson. Only #C90D28.

IV ——— SHAPES

shapes

Six geometric primitives carry the brand's graphic voice. Three are inherited from HybridDot — the dot, the venn, the wave — and three are BloxDot's own: the block grid, the plan line, and the aperture.

Rule of use

Shapes are structural, not decorative. Use one shape per composition. Pair with a single photograph and a single block of type — never three shapes together. Silence is part of the system.

Dot · Plan marker

The brand's signature. A site pin, a period, a promise. Always crimson.

Block grid · Modularity

Nine blocks, one filled. The "blox" in the name: architectural modularity made visible.

Plan line · Measure

A hairline with tick marks — lifted from construction drawings. Used for rules and footers.

Venn · Overlap

Three circles — architecture, interior, craft — meeting as one practice.

Wave · Elevation

Contour lines inside a circle. Topography, site, the ground beneath.

Aperture · Light

Nested squares — an opening through layered walls. The brand's relationship with light.
