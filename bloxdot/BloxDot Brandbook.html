I ——— LOGO

logo

An uppercase geometric wordmark — BLOX — closed by a single crimson dot. The dot is the name, the signature, and the inheritance from our parent studio HybridDot. It is not a period. It is the mark.

Primary lockup

Four glyphs and a dot. The letterforms are a proprietary geometric sans — the stroke widths are constant, the apertures are closed, and the terminals are cut square. The dot is not optional; it completes the mark.

Letterforms

Custom-drawn geometric sans, constructed on a 32-unit height grid. Not a retail typeface — do not substitute. Always use the supplied SVG; never re-set the name in a similar sans.

The dot

A perfect crimson circle positioned at the optical baseline, diameter equal to the letter stroke weight multiplied by three. It reads as punctuation at a glance, as a signature on reflection.

Minimum size

Digital: 88 px wide. Print: 22 mm wide. Below these thresholds the dot loses optical weight — use the monogram (B ·) instead.

File formats

SVG for all digital applications. EPS for print. PNG only where vector is not accepted. Never rasterize below 2× the target resolution.

Clear space — the x rule

Clear space around the wordmark equals the cap-height of the letters (x). Nothing — type, imagery, other marks, page edges — may cross this boundary. The dot counts as part of the mark.

Minimum clear zone= 1 × cap-height

Construction

The wordmark is drawn on a 32-unit cap-height grid. The dot is a perfect circle with diameter equal to 10 units (≈ 0.31 × cap-height) and is offset from the final letter by 3 units. Letter-spacing is fixed; do not tighten or track out.

II ——— COLOR

color

A warm, material palette. Bone and plaster instead of paper-white. Ink instead of pure black. Brass for details. Crimson — inherited from HybridDot — appears only as a signature, never as a field.

Philosophy

BloxDot designs in material: lime plaster, oiled oak, unpolished travertine, oxidised brass. The palette is the same vocabulary translated into pigment. Nothing is pure; everything is honest.

The two-percent rule

Crimson is used sparingly — the dot in the mark, an accent on a date line, an emphasized word. It should not exceed two percent of any composition. A little goes a long way; too much, and the system breaks.

  • The dot of the wordmark. Always, at full size.
  • One accented word per page of body copy, for emphasis.
  • The hairline under a section number, or a tick on a diagram.
  • Never fill a large shape or background with crimson.
  • Never pair crimson directly against brass without ink between.
  • Never use a tint, shade, or gradient of crimson. Only #C90D28.

IV ——— SHAPES

shapes

Six geometric primitives carry the brand's graphic voice. Three are inherited from HybridDot — the dot, the venn, the wave — and three are BloxDot's own: the block grid, the plan line, and the aperture.

Rule of use

Shapes are structural, not decorative. One shape per composition. Pair with a single photograph and a single block of type — never three shapes together. Silence is part of the system.

Dot · Plan marker

The brand's signature. A site pin, a period, a promise. Always crimson, always full-size.

Block grid · Modularity

Nine blocks, one filled. The "blox" in the name: architectural modularity made visible.

Plan line · Measure

A hairline with tick marks — lifted from construction drawings. Used for rules and footers.

Venn · Overlap

Three circles — architecture, interior, craft — meeting as one practice.

Wave · Elevation

Contour lines inside a circle. Topography, site, the ground beneath.

Aperture · Light

Nested squares — an opening through layered walls. The brand's relationship with light.
